Planets of the Solar System

The Solar System consists of the Sun and the objects that orbit it, including eight planets, their moons, and smaller bodies such as dwarf planets, asteroids, and comets.

Mercury

Mercury is the closest planet to the Sun and the smallest planet in the Solar System. It has no atmosphere and experiences extreme temperatures.

Venus

Venus is similar in size to Earth but has a thick, toxic atmosphere that traps heat, making it the hottest planet.

Earth

Earth is the third planet from the Sun and the only known planet to support life, with a diverse climate and abundant water.

Did You Know?

The asteroid belt lies between Mars and Jupiter, containing millions of rocky objects ranging in size from tiny dust particles to dwarf planets like Ceres.

Some comets visit the inner Solar System only once in hundreds or thousands of years.
